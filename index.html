Philosophy of Design

Spaces online are like our own little thoughts, places like our homes that reflect back upon us. We live here, work here, play here...thrive here. The internet is a uniquely human place and should therefore be thoughtfully designed. I design according to ontological design, believing that the spaces around us design us as much as we design them. Design should be an ecosystem of symbiotic harmony.

I also design according to a form of online brutalism. The brutalist architects of decades past believed in designing spaces with only the purest, simplist materials in a way that "got the job done" and was perfect in its utilitarian approach, beautiful in its stark minimalism. I tackle the web in much the same way. I don't believe in heavy JavaScript, AJAX or JQUERY apps meant to replace what should probably have been native. Instead, I believe in using the purest tools of the web to produce simple, minimal plots of land online waiting for your creativity to develop them further.

This is //scratchfive. Designed for people. Designed for the web.
